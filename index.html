Accents

Each of the following accent families were chosen to satisfy the following criteria:

  • Base contrast: The Base variant has at least 4.5:1 contrast with either Gray 100 or Gray 900, and at least 3:1 contrast with the other. This guarantees that the Base can act as a background color without the need for a contrasting border.
  • Extreme contrast: The Light variant has AAA contrast with Gray 900, and the Dark variant has AAA contrast with Gray 100, which ensures that the accents can work even in high-contrast settings.
  • Paired variant contrast: The contrast between the Light and Dark variants is at least 4.5:1, so they can be used against each other. This allows Light variants to be used as a background on Gray 100 if Dark is used as a border, and vice versa on Gray 900.

Note that these colors do not account for any forms of colorblindness. They should not be used for semantic purposes like alerts.

These accent combinations should be perceivable on either Light or Dark backgrounds.
